The Paracas Phase

The paracas phase was during a period in time when an Andean society existing between approximately 800 BCE and 100 BCE,a society that had extensive knowledge of irrigation and water management and that made significant contributions in the textile arts. Most information about the lives of the Paracas people comes from excavations at the large seaside Paracas site on the Paracas Peninsula, are it was investigated in the 1920s by Peruvian archaeologist Julio Tello.
