The History Of The Nazca Lines

Home History Facts Conspiracy

The Nazca Lines are believed to have been created some time between 500 BC and 500 AD with the first mention of the Nazca Lines being in 1553 by Pedro Cieza de León who published a book where he would refer to them as "trail markers" and although at the time the lines were partially visible from nearby hills, the peruvian army and civilian pilots were the first to report them in the 20th century. In 1927 a Peruvian archaeologist named Toribio Mejía Xesspe spotted them while he was out hiking through the foothills.
